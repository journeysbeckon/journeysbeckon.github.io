Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1631 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India

Varanasi Ghats

The Varanasi Ghats are steps leading down to the river Ganges, which is considered holy by Hindus. These ghats are used for various religious activities such as bathing and cremation.

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India

View on Map
Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, or the Palace of Winds, is a palace in Jaipur, India. It is constructed of red and pink sandstone. The palace sits on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and extends to the zenana, or women's chambers.

Jaipur, Rajasthan, India

Hollywood Walk of Fame

The Hollywood Walk of Fame comprises more than 2,600 five-pointed terrazzo and brass stars embedded in the sidewalks along 15 blocks of Hollywood Boulevard and three blocks of Vine Street in Hollywood, California.

Los Angeles, California, United States
